ADALS 2026 · Asia-Pacific dialogue on accessibility, ageing & AI

Call for Speakers

We are looking for practitioners from across the Asia-Pacific to join a working dialogue on accessibility, ageing, and AI.

ADALS 2026 is a first-edition, practitioner-level event. It is built around conversation, not lectures — mid-career practitioners and the people inside intermediary organisations comparing what actually works. If you are doing this work somewhere in the Asia-Pacific, we would like to hear from you.

Before you start

A few things worth knowing before you fill this in:

  • Sessions run in English. ADALS 2026 is a pan-regional dialogue, so the working language is English. You do not need to be a native speaker — you do need to be comfortable presenting and taking part in discussion in English.
  • In person or remote. The event is anchored in Taiwan, but remote participation is genuinely supported. Travel is not a requirement for taking part.
  • This is an expression of interest. Submitting does not confirm a slot. The organising team will be in touch to talk through fit and format.

The programme is built around five themes. Your proposed session should connect to one of them (or tell us it spans several):

  1. Trust & Transparency — opaque decisions, and the right to challenge them as AI scales.
  2. Who Can Use Daily Services? — the shifting threshold to everyday services, and who gets left at the door.
  3. Caregiving and Life in Old Age — care under strain, and whether AI catches the gap or adds to it.
  4. Understanding and Being Understood — communication across ability, language, and generation.
  5. Patches and Rebuilds in Governance — where accessibility sits inside organisations.

On access and captioning. We want this to work for the people taking part. There is a field below for any access needs — please use it. We are also looking into live captioning for sessions; if captions in a particular language would help you or your audience, tell us there. We would rather know early than guess.
